Creator: Yupu Chen

Collaborator: Yoki Xue

Year: 2022

Place: Abu Dhabi, UAE

Keywords: Robot, Physical Computing, Fabrication

This is a robotics project that brought emotions to life—literally and mechanically. Inspired by Disney’s Inside Out, the project was a unique blend of storytelling and engineering, where a team of us built robots to embody the emotions of a student procrastinating on an assignment.

Our version of the story took place in the mind of an NYU Abu Dhabi student, struggling to finish an assignment. Inside her brain, five emotions—anger, joy, sadness, fear, and disgust—interact, each played by robots we designed and built ourselves. To add a twist, we also created two additional robot characters: the student’s imaginary friends and a robot designed to represent her looming assignment.

Development: Joy

I mainly worked on designing and building the robot character for Joy. In Inside Out, Joy is the optimistic and energetic leader of the emotions, so my robot had to convey that same sense of enthusiasm and positivity. To achieve this, I worked on both the mechanics of its movement and its expressive capabilities.

Physical construction of Joy includes using a combination of servo motors and lightweight materials to ensure that Joy could move in a lively and dynamic way. The appearance of Joy features a tall stature and bright colors, designed to mimick the figure in the original Inside Out movie. The robot’s gestures were designed to be fluid and open, reflecting the character’s optimistic nature. Besides asthetics, several praticial considerations also determined the building of Joy. It has a sound rounded base that could support the motors and the weight of the entire boday. The body of Joy was also designed to be a cone shape so that it would not shake heavily when moving fast, which is necessary to convey enthusiatic emotions.

For programming, I used Arduino to control Joy’s movements and interactions. Arduino allowed me to integrate various sensors and motors, ensuring that Joy could react to the environment and other robots in real-time. The movement of Joy was controlled by two motors. The movement of Joy's arms were controlled by two servo motors. For racial expressions, several NeoPixels were used. There were all soldered to Arduino and programmed there.

Performance and Showcasing

Once all the robots were complete, we brought them together for a live performance. Each robot was programmed to represent its corresponding emotion, and together, they mimicked the internal chaos of procrastination following prewritten lines. For Joy, the robot played a critical role in lightening the mood and trying to motivate the student through her struggles.

We showcased this project at the Black Box Theater at NYU Abu Dhabi.

Reflections and Future Steps

This project was an amazing learning experience for me as well as an introductory lesson to Human-Computer Interaction, blending robotics with narrative performance. The performing robot represents an interesting field in HCI, which points to how people could convey emtions through robots and how people receive emotions from robots. Through performance, robots have received an agency of expression that is traditionally believed to be possessed only by human beings. Working on Joy helped me sharpen my skills in robotic engineering, particularly in Arduino programming, motion control, and physical fabrication techniques like soldering, 3D printing, and laser-cutting.

Looking forward, I hope to continue exploring ways in which robotics can merge with art and performance. I also plan to further improve Joy’s movement capabilities, potentially adding more advanced AI features to make her interactions more spontaneous and lifelike.

Conclusion

GalleryEverywhere is an experimental project that explores the ideas of ignorant museums, participatory curation, and augmented reality. Its goal is to make the curation process accessible to everyone so that everyone could make different meanings of museums, galleries, and exhibitions. This project evolved from a Unity3D project to a Swift/SwiftUI project with significant improvements in its performance and UI design. Future development is expected to break the limitation of physical space to allow users to share their own curations with another on the other side of the world.
